The 5 Temples

4 Dhams + the divine wedding site

Dham 1

Yamunotri

Source of the sacred Yamuna River
Goddess Yamuna 3,293 m Opens Apr 19
  • Surya Kund - hot spring where devotees cook rice/potatoes as prasad (nature's kitchen)
  • Divya Shila - sacred rock, puja before entering temple
  • Built by Maharaja Pratap Shah of Tehri Garhwal (19th century)
  • Saptarishi Kund - actual glacial source (12 km from temple - not visiting)

Trek

  • Start: Janki Chatti (last motorable point)
  • Distance: 5-7 km one way
  • Time: 4-5 hrs up, 3-4 hrs down
  • Options: Walk, pony (Rs 2-4K), palki (palanquin)

Darshan: 6:00 AM - 8:00 PM (yatra season)

Dham 2

Gangotri

Where the holy Ganga first touched Earth
Goddess Ganga 3,100 m Opens Apr 19
  • Bhagirathi River - the Ganga begins here as Bhagirathi
  • Built by General Amar Singh Thapa (19th century)
  • Gaumukh Glacier - actual glacial source, 19 km further (not on this trip)
  • No trek required - walk-in temple, accessible by road
  • Harsil Valley - approach road is one of the most stunning in Garhwal

Access

  • Drive from Uttarkashi: 100 km, 3-4 hrs
  • Road goes all the way to the temple
Dham 3 - Jyotirlinga

Kedarnath

One of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva
Lord Shiva 3,583 m Opens Apr 22
  • One of 12 Jyotirlingas in India
  • Adi Shankaracharya Samadhi behind the temple
  • Rebuilt after devastating 2013 floods
  • Snow-capped peaks as backdrop
  • Winter deity moved to Omkareshwar Temple, Ukhimath

Trek

  • Start: Gaurikund (last motorable: Sonprayag, then jeep)
  • Distance: 16 km one way (was 14 km pre-2013, new route longer)
  • Time: 6-8 hrs up, 3-4 hrs down
  • Elevation gain: ~1,500 m
  • Options: Walk, pony (Rs 2-4K), palki, helicopter (Rs 7-8K)

April Weather

  • Avg: 5.6°C | Min: 0.5°C | Max: 10.8°C
  • Light snow cover possible
  • Very cold at night - thermals mandatory
Dham 4 - Divya Desam

Badrinath

The abode of Lord Vishnu, between Nar and Narayan peaks
Lord Vishnu 3,133 m Opens Apr 23
  • One of 108 Divya Desams (Vishnu temples)
  • Tapt Kund - hot spring, holy dip before darshan
  • Neelkanth Peak (6,509 m) as backdrop
  • No trek required - temple accessible by road
  • Banks of Alaknanda River

Mana Village (3 km)

  • India's last village before Tibet
  • Vyas Gufa - cave where Ved Vyas composed the Vedas and Mahabharata
  • Bhim Pul - natural rock bridge over Saraswati River
  • Saraswati River origin - emerges and disappears underground
  • Last chai shop of India
Bonus - Divine Wedding Site

Triyuginarayan

The eternal flame has burned since the divine wedding, across three yugas
Lord Vishnu (Shiva-Parvati wedding) 1,980 m No trek needed

Lord Shiva married Goddess Parvati here. Lord Vishnu performed kanyadaan as Parvati's brother. Lord Brahma was the priest. The sacred fire lit during the wedding has never been extinguished.

  • Akhand Dhuni - eternal flame burning since the divine wedding (across 3 yugas)
  • Brahma Shila - the exact stone where the marriage ceremony happened
  • Three sacred kunds: Rudrakund, Vishnukund, Brahmakund
  • Gauri Cave - 2 km walk, Parvati's meditation spot (optional)
  • Temple architecture mirrors Kedarnath Temple
  • Built/restored by Adi Shankaracharya

Most Char Dham packages skip this entirely. It's a 10-13 km detour from Sonprayag with no trek, and perfectly fills the post-Kedarnath afternoon. 1.5-2 hours is sufficient for full darshan and all kunds.

Risk Assessment & Contingencies

Warning
This plan has zero buffer days. Here's the triage plan.

Scenario Responses

ScenarioAction
Bad weather Day 2Push Yamunotri to Day 3 morning. Gangotri becomes Day 3 afternoon. Tight but possible
Road closure Day 3Skip Gangotri. Drive directly to Uttarkashi, continue to Sonprayag next day
Sick/exhausted Day 5Helicopter up (Rs 7-8K). Trek down next day
Running late Day 6Drop Triyuginarayan. Go straight to Guptkashi
Road closure Day 7Wait it out. Do Badrinath darshan + drive home both on Day 8 (brutal but possible)

Priority Order (if forced to skip)

  1. Triyuginarayan - nice to have, not a Dham
  2. Mana Village - can be skipped on Day 8
  3. Gangotri - if Day 3 fails (but try hard to keep all 4 Dhams)

Key Risks

  • Mountain roads: Landslides, especially early season. Roads can close without warning
  • Altitude sickness: Kedarnath at 3,583 m. Symptoms: headache, nausea, dizziness
  • Opening day crowds: Yamunotri Day 2, Kedarnath Day 5 = heavy traffic
  • Weather: April can bring sudden rain/snow at altitude
  • Vehicle breakdown: Carry driver's mechanic contact. Innova Crysta is reliable but mountains are tough
