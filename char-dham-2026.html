Red Flags - Read This
  • Day 9: Flight at risk. Rudraprayag → Dehradun Airport = 180 km, 5-6 hrs. Flight at 5:20 PM. Must leave by 5:30 AM. One landslide or Rishikesh traffic jam = missed flight. Mitigation: Wake at 4:30 AM. Skip Devprayag/Dhari Devi if running late. Keep driver's number saved. Have Plan B: drive to Rishikesh on Day 8 night if Day 8 runs late
  • Day 5: Hardest day, no pony. Kedarnath trek 16 km uphill on foot = 8-10+ hours. Opening day crowds make it worse. After 4 days of travel your legs will be tired. Mitigation: Start by 5:30-6:00 AM. Walk SLOW. Drink water every 20 min. Carry energy bars. Use trekking pole (non-negotiable). If exhausted at Linchauli (11 km), consider pittu/palki for last 5 km
  • Day 2: Trek + long drive. 12 km Yamunotri trek on foot (8-9 hrs round trip) PLUS 130 km drive to Uttarkashi after. Will reach hotel by 8-9 PM exhausted. Mitigation: Start trek by 5:00 AM sharp. Don't linger at top. Trek down fast while legs are fresh
  • 3 opening days = 3x crowds. Yamunotri (Day 2), Kedarnath (Day 5), Badrinath (Day 7) all fall on opening days. Expect 2-3 hr darshan queues. Mitigation: Join queues early. Be patient. Don't underestimate wait times in schedule

Red Flag - Hardest day, no pony
  • 16 km uphill on foot after 4 days of travel. Your legs will be tired. This is the real test
  • Walk SLOW. Short breaks (2-3 min, not 15-20). Long breaks cool muscles = cramps
  • Drink water every 20 minutes. Dehydration + altitude = disaster
  • Headache/dizziness = altitude sickness. Slow down immediately, don't push through
  • Trekking pole is NON-NEGOTIABLE on foot. Rent at Gaurikund if needed
  • Opening day = crowded trail. Start by 5:30 AM to stay ahead of the crowd
  • Bail-out: If exhausted at Linchauli (11 km), pittu/palki available for last 5 km (Rs 5-7K)

The 4 Dhams

The four sacred abodes of Uttarakhand

Dham 1

Yamunotri

Source of the sacred Yamuna River
Goddess Yamuna 3,293 m Opens Apr 19
  • Surya Kund - hot spring where devotees cook rice/potatoes as prasad (nature's kitchen)
  • Divya Shila - sacred rock, puja before entering temple
  • Built by Maharaja Pratap Shah of Tehri Garhwal (19th century)
  • Saptarishi Kund - actual glacial source (12 km from temple - not visiting)

Trek

  • Start: Jankichatti (last motorable point)
  • Distance: 6 km one way
  • Time: 4-5 hrs up, 3-4 hrs down (on foot)
  • Going on foot - trekking pole recommended

Darshan: 6:00 AM - 8:00 PM (yatra season)

Dham 2

Gangotri

Where the holy Ganga first touched Earth
Goddess Ganga 3,100 m Opens Apr 19
  • Bhagirathi River - the Ganga begins here as Bhagirathi
  • Built by General Amar Singh Thapa (19th century)
  • Gaumukh Glacier - actual glacial source, 19 km further (not on this trip)
  • No trek required - walk-in temple, accessible by road
  • Harsil Valley - approach road is one of the most stunning in Garhwal

Access

  • Drive from Uttarkashi: 100 km, 3-4 hrs
  • Road goes all the way to the temple
Dham 3 - Jyotirlinga

Kedarnath

One of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva
Lord Shiva 3,583 m Opens Apr 22
  • One of 12 Jyotirlingas in India
  • Adi Shankaracharya Samadhi behind the temple
  • Rebuilt after devastating 2013 floods
  • Snow-capped peaks as backdrop
  • Winter deity moved to Omkareshwar Temple, Ukhimath

Trek

  • Start: Gaurikund (last motorable: Sonprayag, then jeep)
  • Distance: 16 km one way (was 14 km pre-2013, new route longer)
  • Time: 8-10+ hrs up on foot. 4-5 hrs down
  • Elevation gain: ~1,600 m
  • Going on foot - trekking pole non-negotiable. Pittu/palki available as bail-out if exhausted

April Weather

  • Avg: 5.6°C | Min: 0.5°C | Max: 10.8°C
  • Light snow cover possible
  • Very cold at night - thermals mandatory
Dham 4 - Divya Desam

Badrinath

The abode of Lord Vishnu, between Nar and Narayan peaks
Lord Vishnu 3,133 m Opens Apr 24
  • One of 108 Divya Desams (Vishnu temples)
  • Tapt Kund - hot spring, holy dip before darshan
  • Neelkanth Peak (6,509 m) as backdrop
  • No trek required - temple accessible by road
  • Banks of Alaknanda River

Mana Village (3 km)

  • India's last village before Tibet
  • Vyas Gufa - cave where Ved Vyas composed the Vedas and Mahabharata
  • Bhim Pul - natural rock bridge over Saraswati River
  • Saraswati River origin - emerges and disappears underground
  • Mata Murti Temple
  • Last chai shop of India

Critical Constraint
Flight at 5:20 PM Sunday from Dehradun. The entire trip must stay on schedule. No buffer days. Every delay cascades.

Key Risks

  • Mountain roads: Landslides, especially early season. Roads can close without warning
  • Altitude sickness: Kedarnath at 3,583 m. Symptoms: headache, nausea, dizziness
